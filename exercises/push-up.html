Push-ups

Chest (Pectorals) Triceps Anterior Deltoids Core Serratus Anterior

The Push-up is the king of bodyweight upper body exercises. When performed with proper form, it's not just a chest exercise — it trains the entire anterior chain while building core stability and scapular control. The key to getting the most from push-ups is treating them as a full-body tension exercise, not just an arm exercise. A well-executed push-up with protraction at the top builds the serratus anterior, which is essential for shoulder health.

Form Cues

  1. Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, fingers spread
  2. Set up in a high plank — body in a straight line from head to heels
  3. Engage your core and squeeze your glutes — no sagging or piking
  4. Lower yourself by bending the elbows to about 45° from your body (not flared)
  5. Touch your chest to the ground (or close to it)
  6. Press up by driving through your palms
  7. At the top, push a little extra — protract your shoulder blades
  8. Control the tempo: 2 seconds down, 1 second up

Common Mistakes

  • Sagging hips (weak core engagement)
  • Flaring elbows out to 90° — keep them at ~45°
  • Not going through full range of motion
  • "Snake" push-ups where hips and chest don't move together
  • Forgetting to protract at the top

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Incline push-ups on a bench or wall
Harder
Deficit push-ups, archer push-ups, weighted push-ups
