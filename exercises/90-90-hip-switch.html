90/90 Hip Switches

Hip Internal Rotators Hip External Rotators Glutes Adductors

The 90/90 Hip Switch is a ground-based hip mobility drill where both legs are bent at 90 degrees — one in external rotation, one in internal rotation. Switching between sides trains the full rotational capacity of the hips. This exercise is widely used in FRC, physical therapy, and mobility programs because it effectively develops the internal rotation that most adults lack. Good hip internal rotation is essential for healthy knees, a pain-free lower back, and fluid athletic movement.

Form Cues

  1. Sit on the floor with both knees bent at 90 degrees
  2. Front leg: shin parallel to your chest, knee in external rotation
  3. Back leg: shin behind you, knee in internal rotation
  4. Sit tall — don't slump or round your back
  5. Rotate both legs simultaneously to switch sides
  6. The front leg becomes the back leg and vice versa
  7. Try to keep both sit bones on the ground throughout
  8. Move smoothly and controlled — don't use momentum

Common Mistakes

  • Leaning back or rounding the spine
  • Lifting the sit bones off the ground during switches
  • Moving too quickly and using momentum
  • Not achieving full 90-degree angles at the knees
  • Ignoring the side that feels tighter

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Use hands behind you for support, reduce range
Harder
Hands-free switches, add a forward fold over the front shin
