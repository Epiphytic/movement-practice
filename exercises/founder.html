The Founder

Posterior Chain Glutes Hamstrings Erector Spinae

The Founder is the cornerstone exercise of Foundation Training, created by Dr. Eric Goodman. It's a powerful standing decompression exercise that strengthens the entire posterior chain while teaching your body to hinge properly at the hips. By anchoring your weight back and lifting your chest, you create length and tension through the spine.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, weight in heels
  2. Hinge at the hips, pushing your butt back as if sitting into a chair
  3. Keep your chest lifted and spine long — do NOT round your back
  4. Extend arms forward, then sweep them behind you
  5. Pull your shoulders back and down, squeezing shoulder blades together
  6. Hold with weight anchored in your heels, knees slightly bent
  7. Breathe deeply — expand your ribcage with each inhale
  8. Stand tall to finish, driving through the heels

Common Mistakes

  • Rounding the lower back instead of hinging at hips
  • Letting knees drift forward past toes
  • Holding breath instead of breathing deeply
  • Shrugging shoulders up toward ears
  • Not pushing weight back into heels

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Reduce hold time to 15-20 seconds while you build strength and endurance in the position.
Harder
Add arm reach overhead and extend holds to 60+ seconds for greater posterior chain demand.
