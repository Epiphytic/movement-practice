Woodpecker

Hamstrings Glutes Erector Spinae Core (Balance)

The Woodpecker is a single-leg hip hinge from Foundation Training that builds posterior chain strength and balance simultaneously. Named for the bird's characteristic forward-tilting motion, this exercise trains you to hinge on one leg while maintaining a rigid, straight line from head to trailing foot. It's exceptional for identifying and correcting asymmetries between sides.

Form Cues

  1. Stand on one leg, slight bend in the standing knee
  2. Hinge forward at the hip, extending the free leg straight behind you
  3. Keep your hips level — don't let the free hip rotate open
  4. Reach arms forward to counterbalance
  5. Create one straight line from fingertips through your spine to your trailing foot
  6. Hold briefly at the bottom, squeezing the standing glute
  7. Return to standing by driving through the standing heel
  8. Complete all reps on one side before switching

Common Mistakes

  • Rounding the back instead of hinging at the hip
  • Letting the hip of the free leg rotate open
  • Bending the trailing knee
  • Not maintaining core tension throughout
  • Rushing through reps — this is a slow, controlled movement

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Keep trailing toe on the ground as a kickstand for added balance support.
Harder
Hold a light weight in your hands and add a pause at the bottom of each rep.
