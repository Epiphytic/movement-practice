Integrated Hinge

Posterior Chain Glutes Hamstrings Adductors Erector Spinae

The Integrated Hinge is an advanced Foundation Training exercise that combines the hip hinge pattern with adductor engagement and spinal decompression. It takes the principles of The Founder and adds a wider stance, creating a powerful full-body integration exercise. This movement builds tremendous posterior chain endurance and teaches the body to use the glutes and hamstrings as primary movers in the hinge pattern.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, toes turned slightly out
  2. Hinge at the hips, pushing your butt back
  3. Keep your chest up and spine long — maintain a neutral spine
  4. Reach arms forward, then sweep them back alongside your body
  5. Squeeze your inner thighs (adductors) as if pulling the floor together
  6. Engage glutes powerfully — this is a full posterior chain contraction
  7. Hold the hinged position while breathing deeply
  8. Drive through the heels to return to standing

Common Mistakes

  • Rounding the back during the hinge
  • Not engaging the adductors — missing the "integrated" part
  • Standing too narrow (this needs a wider stance than The Founder)
  • Holding breath instead of breathing through the hold
  • Letting knees cave inward

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Narrower stance, shorter holds
Harder
Longer holds (60+ sec), add arm variations overhead
