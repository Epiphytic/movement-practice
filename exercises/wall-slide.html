Wall Slides

Serratus Anterior Lower Trapezius Rotator Cuff Thoracic Spine

Wall Slides are a shoulder health exercise that trains scapular upward rotation and overhead mobility against a wall. They strengthen the serratus anterior and lower traps — the muscles responsible for healthy, pain-free overhead movement. Wall Slides also improve thoracic spine extension and help counteract the forward-rounded posture from desk work. They're simple but surprisingly challenging when done correctly.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with your back flat against a wall, feet 6 inches from the wall
  2. Press your lower back, upper back, and head against the wall
  3. Place arms against the wall in a "goalpost" position (elbows at 90°)
  4. Press the backs of your hands, wrists, and elbows into the wall
  5. Slowly slide your arms up the wall, straightening the elbows
  6. Maintain contact with the wall through the entire range
  7. Slide back down to the starting position with control
  8. Keep your core engaged and ribs down throughout

Common Mistakes

  • Lower back arching away from the wall (keep it pressed flat)
  • Arms losing contact with the wall — especially the wrists
  • Shrugging the shoulders up instead of keeping them depressed
  • Moving too fast — this should be slow and controlled
  • Not pressing actively into the wall (just letting arms float)

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Floor slides (lying face-up, same movement)
Harder
Add a light resistance band around wrists, slow the tempo further
