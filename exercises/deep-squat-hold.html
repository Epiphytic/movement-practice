Deep Squat Hold

Quads Glutes Adductors Ankle Dorsiflexors Hip Flexors Core

The Deep Squat Hold (also called the Asian Squat or Third World Squat) is the most natural resting position for humans — yet most Western adults have lost the ability to do it comfortably. Spending time in a deep squat restores ankle mobility, hip flexibility, and pelvic floor function. It decompresses the spine, aids digestion, and maintains the joint ranges you need for healthy aging. The goal is to eventually hold this position comfortably for minutes at a time.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider, toes turned out 15-30 degrees
  2. Slowly lower yourself into a full squat — hips below knees
  3. Keep your heels flat on the ground (use a wedge or plates if needed at first)
  4. Push your knees out over your toes
  5. Keep your chest up and spine as neutral as possible
  6. Hold your hands in front for counterbalance if needed
  7. Breathe deeply and try to relax into the position
  8. Gently shift your weight side to side to explore the range

Common Mistakes

  • Heels lifting off the ground (address ankle mobility first)
  • Excessive forward lean / rounding of the back
  • Knees caving inward instead of tracking over toes
  • Holding breath or tensing up — try to relax into it
  • Giving up too early — build hold time gradually

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Hold a doorframe or pole, elevate heels on a wedge
Harder
Weighted goblet squat hold, add squat-to-stand transitions
