Lunge Decompression

Hip Flexors Posterior Chain Thoracic Spine Psoas

The Lunge Decompression is a Foundation Training exercise that combines a deep lunge position with spinal decompression. It targets the hip flexors and psoas while creating space and length through the spine. This exercise is particularly beneficial for anyone who sits for extended periods, as it opens the front of the hip while strengthening the posterior chain. It flows naturally from The Founder into the Integrated Hinge.

Form Cues

  1. Step into a deep lunge position, back knee on or near the ground
  2. Keep your torso upright — don't lean forward
  3. Anchor your pelvis by engaging your lower abs and glutes
  4. Raise your arms overhead, reaching toward the ceiling
  5. Create length through the spine — imagine being pulled upward
  6. Breathe deeply, expanding the ribcage with each inhale
  7. Feel the stretch through the hip flexor of the back leg
  8. Hold and breathe, then switch sides

Common Mistakes

  • Leaning the torso forward instead of staying upright
  • Losing pelvic control — ribs flaring, lower back arching
  • Not breathing deeply enough during the hold
  • Rushing through the position instead of holding and breathing
  • Letting the front knee collapse inward

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Keep back knee on a pad, hands on hips
Harder
Deepen the lunge, add lateral reaches while in position
