Crocodile Breathing

Diaphragm Pelvic Floor Deep Core Stabilizers Intercostals

Crocodile Breathing is a foundational breathing drill performed face-down on the floor. The prone position provides feedback — you can feel your belly pressing into the ground with each breath, ensuring you're breathing diaphragmatically rather than with your chest. This exercise activates the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest), reduces stress hormones, and trains the deep core stabilizers. It's the perfect recovery exercise and an excellent way to down-regulate after a workout or stressful day.

Form Cues

  1. Lie face-down on the floor
  2. Stack your hands and rest your forehead on them
  3. Legs relaxed, toes pointed outward
  4. Breathe in through your nose for 4 counts
  5. Feel your belly push into the floor — NOT your chest rising
  6. Feel your lower back rise and your sides expand
  7. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6-8 counts
  8. Pause briefly at the bottom of the exhale before the next breath
  9. Each breath should be 360° — belly, sides, and low back all expand

Common Mistakes

  • Breathing into the chest instead of the belly
  • Breathing too fast — this should be slow and meditative
  • Tensing up — let your entire body relax into the floor
  • Not pausing between breaths
  • Quitting too early — 5 minutes creates a real physiological shift

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Just focus on belly breathing for the first few sessions
Harder
Extend exhale duration, add breath holds, transition to quadruped breathing
