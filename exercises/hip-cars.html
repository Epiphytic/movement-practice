Hip CARs

Hip Joint Glutes Hip Flexors Adductors Abductors Deep Hip Rotators

Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations) are focused rotations of the hip joint through its complete range of motion. Unlike the full-body CARs routine, these are performed with maximum intent and irradiation, spending dedicated time on each hip. Hip CARs are essential for maintaining hip joint health, preventing degeneration, and improving mobility for squatting, lunging, and everyday movement. They also serve as a daily assessment — any change in your available range tells you something about your hip health.

Form Cues

  1. Stand tall on one leg, holding a wall or chair for balance
  2. Create full-body tension — squeeze your standing glute, brace your core
  3. Lift the working knee up in front of you as high as possible
  4. Rotate the knee out to the side (external rotation)
  5. Continue the circle by bringing the knee behind you (extension)
  6. Complete the full circle back to the starting position
  7. Move as slowly as possible — take 30+ seconds per rotation
  8. Reverse direction and repeat
  9. Switch legs and repeat both directions

Common Mistakes

  • Moving too fast — hip CARs should be extremely slow
  • Compensating with the pelvis or lower back
  • Not creating enough full-body tension
  • Making small circles instead of exploring the full range
  • Leaning excessively to one side

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Hands and knees (quadruped) hip CARs
Harder
Standing without support, add PAILs/RAILs at end ranges
