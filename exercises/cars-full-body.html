Full Body CARs

Neck Shoulders Spine Hips Ankles Wrists

Controlled Articular Rotations (CARs) are the foundation of the Functional Range Conditioning (FRC) system developed by Dr. Andreo Spina. CARs involve slowly rotating each joint through its fullest available range of motion with maximal irradiation (full-body tension). This daily practice maintains and expands joint health, provides a daily self-assessment of mobility, and sends critical signals to your nervous system to preserve range of motion.

Form Cues

  1. Create full-body tension — squeeze fists, brace core, engage glutes
  2. Isolate the target joint — move ONLY that joint, nothing else moves
  3. Move slowly through the largest circle possible
  4. At each "sticky point" or restriction, pause briefly and breathe into it
  5. Reverse direction and repeat
  6. Move through joints in order: neck → shoulders → spine → hips → ankles → wrists
  7. Spend more time on joints that feel restricted
  8. Maintain tension throughout — this is strength training for your joints

Common Mistakes

  • Moving too fast — CARs should be slow and deliberate
  • Compensating with other body parts (e.g., moving the whole torso during shoulder CARs)
  • Not creating enough full-body tension/irradiation
  • Skipping the reverse direction
  • Rushing through restricted ranges instead of exploring them

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Perform seated CARs with less irradiation to reduce the full-body tension demand.
Harder
Increase irradiation intensity and add PAILs/RAILs at end range to build strength at your limits.
