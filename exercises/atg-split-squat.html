ATG Split Squat

Quads VMO Hip Flexors Glutes Tibialis Anterior

The ATG (Ass-To-Grass) Split Squat, popularized by Ben Patrick (Knees Over Toes Guy), is a full-range split squat where the back knee touches the ground and the front knee travels well past the toes. This exercise strengthens the knee through a full range of motion, develops bulletproof tendons, and builds the VMO (inner quad) and hip flexors. It's a foundational lower body exercise for long-term knee health.

Form Cues

  1. Start in a split stance, back foot elevated on toes
  2. Lower slowly, allowing the back knee to descend toward the ground
  3. Let the front knee travel forward past the toes — this is intentional and safe
  4. Back knee should gently touch the ground (or as low as comfortable)
  5. Drive up through the front heel and midfoot
  6. Keep torso upright throughout
  7. Control the descent — take 3 seconds to lower
  8. Full range of motion is the goal; reduce load if you can't hit depth

Common Mistakes

  • Not going deep enough — partial reps miss the benefit
  • Leaning the torso too far forward
  • Rushing the descent instead of controlling it
  • Front heel lifting off the ground
  • Ignoring pain — some discomfort is ok, sharp pain is not

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Hold onto a pole or doorframe for balance and reduce depth until strength and mobility improve.
Harder
Hold dumbbells at your sides or elevate the front foot for a deficit split squat to increase range of motion.
