Cossack Squat

Adductors Quads Glutes Hip Mobility Ankle Mobility

The Cossack Squat is a lateral squat where you shift your weight fully to one side while extending the other leg straight out. It develops frontal plane strength, deep hip mobility, and adductor flexibility simultaneously. This movement is invaluable for athletes and anyone seeking well-rounded lower body function. It builds the lateral stability and mobility that bilateral squats miss.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with feet wide — about 1.5-2x shoulder width
  2. Shift your weight to one side, bending that knee deeply
  3. The opposite leg extends straight, toes pointing up
  4. Keep the working heel flat on the ground
  5. Sit as deep as possible — ideally hamstring to calf
  6. Keep your chest up and torso as upright as possible
  7. Drive through the working heel to return to center
  8. Alternate sides with control

Common Mistakes

  • Heel lifting on the working side (ankle mobility issue)
  • Straight leg foot rolling onto its side
  • Rounding the back excessively
  • Not going deep enough — partial range limits the benefit
  • Rushing and using momentum to come out of the bottom

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Hold a counterweight (goblet position) or TRX for balance, reduce depth
Harder
Hold weight overhead, add pause at bottom, perform as a flow without returning to center
