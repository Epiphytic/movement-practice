Anchored Back Extension

Erector Spinae Posterior Chain Thoracic Spine Diaphragm

The Anchored Back Extension is a Foundation Training decompression exercise that creates space between the vertebrae while strengthening the muscles that support your spine. By anchoring your pelvis and extending through the thoracic spine, you counteract the compression that comes from sitting and daily life. Combined with deep diaphragmatic breathing, this exercise is powerfully restorative.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart, knees slightly bent
  2. Anchor your pelvis — tuck slightly and engage your lower abs
  3. Place hands behind your head, elbows wide
  4. Lift and extend your chest toward the ceiling
  5. Create length through your entire spine — imagine space between each vertebra
  6. Take a deep breath in, expanding your ribcage laterally
  7. Exhale slowly while maintaining the extension
  8. Repeat for 3 full breath cycles

Common Mistakes

  • Hyperextending the lower back instead of the thoracic spine
  • Losing the pelvic anchor (letting the ribs flare)
  • Shallow breathing — this exercise requires deep, expansive breaths
  • Crunching the neck backward

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Place hands on your lower back for support while learning the pelvic anchor and thoracic extension.
Harder
Extend arms overhead during holds to increase the demand on the posterior chain and thoracic extensors.
