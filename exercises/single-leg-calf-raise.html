Single Leg Calf Raise

Gastrocnemius Soleus Achilles Tendon

The Single Leg Calf Raise is a fundamental lower leg exercise that builds calf strength and Achilles tendon resilience. Performing calf raises one leg at a time ensures you address strength imbalances between sides. This exercise is essential for ankle stability, running performance, and injury prevention. Ben Patrick recommends working toward full range of motion — from a deep stretch at the bottom to a full contraction at the top.

Form Cues

  1. Stand on the edge of a step or raised surface on one foot
  2. Let your heel drop below the step for a full stretch at the bottom
  3. Push up onto your toes as high as possible
  4. Squeeze the calf at the top and hold for 1 second
  5. Lower slowly (3 seconds) back to the stretched position
  6. Keep your knee straight (or slightly bent to target soleus)
  7. Hold a wall or rail for balance only — don't pull yourself up
  8. Complete all reps on one side before switching

Common Mistakes

  • Bouncing at the bottom instead of controlling the stretch
  • Not using full range of motion (partial reps)
  • Going too fast — the eccentric (lowering) phase is critical
  • Using the hand rail to assist the lift

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Two-leg calf raises, flat ground
Harder
Hold a dumbbell, add a pause at the bottom stretch
