Tibialis Raise

Tibialis Anterior Ankle Stabilizers

The Tibialis Raise strengthens the tibialis anterior — the muscle on the front of your shin. This often-neglected muscle is critical for knee health, ankle stability, and athletic performance. Ben Patrick (Knees Over Toes Guy) considers this exercise foundational for building bulletproof knees. Strong tibialis muscles help decelerate the body, protect the knees during impact, and balance the strength of the calves.

Form Cues

  1. Stand with your back against a wall, feet about 12 inches forward
  2. Lean your weight into the wall
  3. Lift your toes and forefeet off the ground as high as possible
  4. Squeeze the tibialis (front of shin) at the top
  5. Lower slowly with control — don't just drop your toes
  6. Keep your heels planted firmly on the ground throughout
  7. Aim for full dorsiflexion at the top of each rep
  8. Perform all reps with control — no bouncing

Common Mistakes

  • Not getting full range of motion at the top
  • Dropping the toes too fast on the descent
  • Feet too close to the wall (reduces range)
  • Using momentum instead of muscular control

Progressions & Regressions

Easier
Perform seated tibialis raises to reduce the bodyweight demand while still strengthening the tibialis anterior.
Harder
Progress to single-leg tibialis raises or add a Tib Bar for loaded raises with progressive overload.
